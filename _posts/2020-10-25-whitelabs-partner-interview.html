Since when have you been following the Kombucha market and why?

Joanne: Entering the kombucha market was a natural fit for our company due to our expertise and understanding of beverage fermentation. As Kombucha became more popular in the US market, we started to receive inquiries from producers to assist them with both regulatory and QC questions. So we started offering specific Kombucha analysis packages and then started to produce a commercial SCOBY culture.
Kara: When I started at White Labs in 2009, a grocery store chain in the states had to recall much of the kombucha on the shelves due to high alcohol percentages. We immediately became a resource for kombucha makers, helping them troubleshoot their fermentation issues so they could comply with regulations and create better products.

What role does your business want to play in the Kombucha market with its products?

Our analytical lab provides an array of analysis for your SCOBY and kombucha. We can provide microbiological analysis, acid percentages, alcohol content, sugars and more. Several years ago, we started offering a lab certified SCOBY culture.
