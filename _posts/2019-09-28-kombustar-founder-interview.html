Andy Heerwagen
Founder of KombuStar Kombucha

Andy Heerwagen is the founder of KombuStar Kombucha. Actually, I am an engineer for biotechnology and have already a good deal of experiences in fermentation from my professional life.

When my family and I was living in the USA, we got in touch with fermented foods especially with Kombucha. Discovering that Kombucha was supporting gut health, I was deeply moved about it and its inherent functional benefits. I already knew Kombucha from my childhood back in the day, but when I got to know it in the USA once again I was thrilled.

During this time my mother was suffering from the side effects of diabetes. I thought Kombucha could help to improve her condition. I got started in my kitchen with the first batch of Kombucha. Furthermore, I visited some breweries in California to learn more about the manufacturing of Kombucha and dove deeper into the science behind probiotics and their impact on the gut health and the microbiome. Back in Cologne, I founded KombuStar Kombucha with the aim of creating an optimal composition of probiotic effectiveness and the best taste experience. When I provided friends, family and my co- athletes/bicyclists with my composition the feedback was overwhelming â€“ and my Star was born.

What excites you most about Kombucha and its future?

When I discovered Kombucha a true passion starts instantly and became a full-grown love. I believe that Kombucha is way more than a lifestyle beverage or functional food. Probiotics support the microbiome and are supposed to help with many diseases related to the gut. The research on the gut as an underestimated organ and its impact to the whole organism is just at the beginning. The more we will be knowing about the microbiome, e.g. the interaction between gut and brain, and many more effects, the more people will be getting aware of a gut-sane diet. The gut is the shooting star, Kombucha the rock star.
